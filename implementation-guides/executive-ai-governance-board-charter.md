# Executive AI Governance Board Charter

## Purpose

The Executive AI Governance Board ensures that AI systems are visible, owned, risk-classified, evidence-based, controlled, monitored, corrected, and stoppable.

## Operating Law

No AI system moves faster than ownership, evidence, authority, and control.

## Scope

The board governs:

- High-risk AI systems
- Critical AI systems
- Frontier AI systems
- Vendor AI with significant impact
- Autonomous agents with sensitive permissions
- Public-impact AI systems
- AI incidents and shutdown decisions

## Membership

Recommended members:

- Executive sponsor
- Business owner representative
- Chief information officer or technology lead
- Chief information security officer or security lead
- Legal/compliance lead
- Privacy/data governance lead
- Risk management lead
- Operational owner representatives
- Internal audit observer where appropriate

## Responsibilities

- Approve High, Critical, and Frontier AI deployments
- Review AI risk tier assignments
- Require evidence before approval
- Enforce human authority requirements
- Enforce least privilege for autonomous agents
- Review vendor AI accountability
- Oversee incident response
- Approve shutdown and restart decisions
- Track governance metrics

## Decision Types

- Approve
- Approve with restrictions
- Pilot only
- Require more evidence
- Reject
- Pause
- Shut down
- Retire

## Meeting Cadence

- Monthly during initial rollout
- Quarterly after stabilization
- Emergency session for Critical or Frontier incidents

## Metrics

- Number of inventoried AI systems
- Percentage with named owners
- Percentage risk-classified
- High+ systems with evidence complete
- Vendor reviews completed
- Incidents reported
- Shutdown paths tested
- Systems paused, restricted, or retired
