# Executive AI Governance Board Charter

## Purpose

The Executive AI Governance Board provides enterprise-level oversight for AI systems, risks, approvals, incidents, and shutdown decisions.

## Authority

The board may:

- Approve or reject high-risk AI deployments
- Require additional evidence
- Restrict AI use
- Suspend AI systems
- Require vendor remediation
- Escalate critical risks to the CEO, board, or public authority where required
- Approve restart after shutdown

## Membership

Recommended members:

- Executive sponsor
- Chief Information Security Officer
- Chief Privacy Officer
- General Counsel
- Chief Risk Officer
- Chief Data or AI Officer
- Business unit executives
- Procurement lead
- Compliance lead
- Internal audit lead

## Standing Agenda

- AI inventory status
- High-risk deployment requests
- Critical vendor risks
- Evidence gaps
- Incidents and corrective actions
- Shutdown decisions
- Regulatory or policy changes
- Training and adoption status
- Department readiness

## Decision Rule

No AI system moves faster than ownership, evidence, authority, and control.
