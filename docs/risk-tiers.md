# Risk Tiers

Risk tiers determine the level of review, evidence, control, and approval required before deployment.

## Low

Minimal operational or public impact.

Examples:

- Internal drafting support
- Non-sensitive summarization
- Low-impact productivity support

Approval level: Team owner

## Moderate

Meaningful business, user, or workflow impact.

Examples:

- Internal decision support
- Customer service support with human review
- Non-critical workflow automation

Approval level: Business owner

## High

Impacts people, rights, money, safety, security, operations, or public trust.

Examples:

- Hiring support
- Benefits support
- Financial recommendations
- Security triage
- Public-facing decision support

Approval level: Executive owner

## Critical

Mission-critical, safety-critical, national-security, legal, rights-sensitive, or infrastructure-sensitive use.

Examples:

- Healthcare triage support
- Critical infrastructure operations
- National security analytics
- Legal or compliance decision support
- Safety-critical automation

Approval level: Executive governance board

## Frontier

Advanced autonomous, high-capability, hard-to-control, or difficult-to-evaluate AI systems.

Examples:

- Autonomous agents with external system access
- Systems with self-directed tool use
- Highly capable models operating in sensitive environments
- Systems that can materially alter cyber, biological, physical, financial, or public information risk

Approval level: Board-level review
