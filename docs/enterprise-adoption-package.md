# Enterprise Adoption Package

The enterprise adoption package exists to make AI governance operational across a real organization.

## Enterprise Adoption Problem

Most organizations do not fail at AI governance because they lack principles.

They fail because:

- AI systems are not fully inventoried
- Owners are unclear
- Evidence is inconsistent
- Vendor accountability is incomplete
- Monitoring is fragmented
- Incident response is improvised
- Shutdown paths are not tested
- Executives lack a reliable view of AI risk

## Enterprise Adoption Objective

The objective is to create a repeatable governance operating rhythm.

Every department should be able to answer:

- What AI systems are we using?
- Who owns them?
- What risk tier are they?
- What evidence supports deployment?
- What controls are active?
- What vendors are involved?
- What incidents occurred?
- What systems can be stopped?
- What requires executive decision?

## Enterprise Adoption Model

1. Establish executive mandate.
2. Create an AI Governance Board.
3. Inventory AI systems.
4. Assign owners.
5. Classify risk.
6. Build evidence binders.
7. Review vendors.
8. Train stakeholders.
9. Monitor and report.
10. Correct or shut down unsafe systems.

## Success Standard

AI governance is working when executives can see the AI estate, identify risk, assign accountability, and make timely decisions before failures scale.
